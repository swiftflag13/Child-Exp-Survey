The Complete State Mental Model of Childhood Experiences

Greetings! This survey blends two well-researched approaches to help give you a unique perspective on your experiences during your childhood.

The complete state model of mental health frames psychological functioning along two distinct but related dimensions by evaluating a child’s condition based on both the presence of positive well-being along with the presence of mental illness or difficulties. This framework blends indicators of mental health challenges – in this case, this is measured by the Adverse Childhood Experiences Survey (ACES)—along with measures of psychological well-being, such as the Benevolent Childhood Experiences Survey (BCES) used in this survey. By assessing these factors simultaneously, the model categorizes youth into states ranging from "flourishing" (few mental health issues and high levels of positive functioning) to "struggling" (many mental health issues and low levels of positive functioning), acknowledging that a child may be free of mental illness yet still lack the positive mental health necessary for optimal development.

At the Center for Men’s Excellence, we see the importance of getting a clear sense of areas of difficulty – what’s “wrong” with a client – along with an equally clear sense of what’s “right” with someone. It’s essential to understand these multiple dimensions of anyone when working to support them in a way that results in lasting change and self-reliance.

So, we invite you to complete this brief survey to see which category of the mental health continua your childhood experiences map onto. Please note the following:

  1. This is a free, automated option – not psychotherapy, assessment, or a diagnosis
  2. Your responses are completely anonymous, and are not saved in our system
